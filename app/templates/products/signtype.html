How it works

SignType tokenizes input text into words and looks each up in a pre-built sign animation library. Known words play their recorded motion capture clip; unknown words fall back to fingerspelling. A lightweight NLP preprocessor handles contractions, numbers, and common phrase shortcuts (e.g., "don't know" → the ASL idiom rather than three separate signs).

The avatar is rendered with Three.js on a WebGL canvas. Skeleton data is streamed frame-by-frame from an animation queue, with IK-solved transitions between signs to prevent jarring joint snaps. The result feels natural and readable at comfortable signing speeds.
